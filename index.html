KALPANA CHAWLA



ABOUT KALPANA CHAWLA

☛ Chawla was born on March 17, 1962, in Karnal, Haryana, India.



☛ She earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from Punjab Engineering College, Chandigarh, and then went on to complete her master's and doctorate in aerospace engineering from the University of Texas and the University of Colorado, respectively.

☛ In 1994, she joined NASA as a NASA astronaut and became the first woman of Indian origin in space.

☛ She made her first space flight in 1997 aboard the Space Shuttle Columbia on mission STS-87.

☛ In 2003, she was on board the Space Shuttle Columbia for her second flight, STS-107. Unfortunately, during re-entry, the shuttle disintegrated, and Kalpana Chawla, along with the other six crew members, tragically lost their lives.

☛ Kalpana Chawla's legacy continues to inspire generations of young people, especially women, to pursue careers in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics.

☛ In honor of her contributions, the Kalpana Chawla Award was established by the Government of India, and numerous schools, institutions, and spaceships have been named after her.

☛ Kalpana's motto was always to "Follow your dreams," and she achieved greatness by breaking barriers and following her passion for aerospace and space exploration.
